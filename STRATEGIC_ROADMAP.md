## 2. ðŸ“Š SWOT Analysis

### Strengths (Internal - Our Superpowers)
*   **The "TPU" Architecture:** Running advanced AI logic on 512MB RAM servers (via our localized "Clarity Engine"). 100x cheaper operational costs than competitors using standard cloud GPUs.
*   **Zero Data Entry:** The "Photo-to-Data" pipeline is our moat. We don't ask users to type; we ask them to snap a photo.
*   **Agentic Framework:** We offer "Digital Employees" (Bursar, Director) that proactively manage the school, unlike passive databases.
*   **Offline Tolerance:** The PWA architecture allows full functionality during internet blackouts, syncing when connectivity returns.

### Weaknesses (Internal - To Fix)
*   **Inclusivity Gap:** Lack of USSD/SMS fallback for the ~40% of parents with feature phones (stupid phones).
*   **Integration Gaps:** No direct hook into UNEB (National Exams) or Mobile Money APIs yet (manual reconciliation still required).
*   **Onboarding Friction:** Migrating existing data is still 85% automated; the last 15% (weird Excel formats) hurts adoption.

### Opportunities (External - The Gold Rush)
*   **Y Combinator & SVC:** "AI for Emerging Markets" is a top investment thesis. We fit the "Leapfrog" narrative (skipping PC era directly to AI).
*   **Data Monetization:** Anonymized data on student health/attendance is gold for NGOs (Gates Foundation) and Governments.
*   **Hardware Leasing:** Bundle cheap $50 Android tablets with Angels AI pre-installed to lock in schools.

### Threats (External - The Risks)
*   **Regulatory Pivot:** Governments mandating state-owned EMIS (Education Management Information Systems) and banning private data silos. *Mitigation: Become the API layer that feeds the Govt EMIS.*
*   **Telco Encroachment:** MTN/Airtel offering "School Pay" with free data. *Mitigation: Build on top of them, don't fight them.*
